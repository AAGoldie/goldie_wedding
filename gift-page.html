Making memories

As we have lived together for many happy years now we would love to make some amazing exciting memories on our honeymoon.

We don't expect anything from our guests but if your intention is to contribute to our day, we would love to put contributions towards all the amazing things that Cancun has to offer.

We've already looked out all the things we'd love to do on our time away (gives us something to be excited about with over a year of lockdown!)

Akumal Diving

Multi-level diving off the coast of Akumal. We've never done anything like this before so would love to see all the underwater wild life Cancun has to offer.

Chichen Itza Day Trip

Guided tour through the hallowed grounds of the ancient Mayan city and additional world-class landmarks like the iconic Temple of Kukulcan, the Observatory, more pyramids and other ancient temples.

Tulum Ruins & Xel-Ha Day Trip

An archaeological and eco-park adventure during an all-inclusive Xel-Ha and Tulum excursion! This unique outing gives you the chance to get up close and personal with the walled Mayan city of Tulum, as well as added rope swings, jungle treks, snorkeling and more at the amazing Xel-Ha park.

Xcaret - Festival of Life & Death

Walk through the arch made of cempasuchil flowers and absorb the aromas, colors and traditions of the Day of the Dead.

Every year, Xcaret gathers the rituals of the Mayan world and the traditions of a guest state to commemorate a celebration declared Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity by UNESCO.

Coba Pacchen Day Trip

Immerse ourselves into the Mayan tradition and encounter Coba, an archaeological site housing the tallest pyramid in the Yucatan Peninsula.

This excursion takes you to a Mayan village to zip line, rappel, canoe and swim around a cenote (freshwater sinkhole) 55 feet deep into the jungle’s ground. An ancient Mayan ceremony and a traditional lunch complete a full day you wish wouldn’t end.

Xplor Park

Two seven-zip-line-circuits including the highest in the destination, two amphibious vehicle courses crossing through the roads of the jungle and flooded caverns; a unique zip-line where you’ll be riding on a hammock and splash on a refreshing cenote, underground rivers to swim or paddle across stalactite sceneries, and a spelunking hike to reach every activity.
